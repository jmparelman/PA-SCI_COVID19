Recent changes to the Pennsylvania Department of Corrections’ management and reporting of COVID-19 testing data have dramatically affected our ability to report the most complete and representative information about the spread of COVID-19 in Pennsylvania prisons.

The DOC has severely restricted our access to vital information necessary for our organization’s efforts to communicate COVID-19 testing data on this website.

In the absence of complete information, we continue to use what information the DOC is still making available. This website has undergone significant changes to communicate the available data. We are no longer able to present information about the number of tests or positive tests in PA prisons. The primary piece of information we do have is an estimate of the number of active cases in PA prisons (inmate active positive cases and asymptomatic inmate positive cases combined), and we now display this as the main figure on this website.

We are continuing to pressure the DOC for the release of complete COVID-19 testing data, and will return this website to its original state when this data is again available.

Pennsylvania Prison System COVID-19 Tracker

Since the beginning of the pandemic the Amistad Law Project has tracked COVID-19's spread through the Pennsylvania state prison system and advocated for a dramatic reduction of the prison population to reduce the harm of the virus to incarcerated people and staff. Prisons are high-risk environments, often overcrowded and poorly ventilated where social distancing is impossible. The lack of care of incarcerated people by the state must be monitored, understood and chalenged as this pandemic continues to impact this vulnerable population. It is within the Governor’s power to expand his reprieve order and reduce the prison population so people can single cell and social distance, and in doing so save lives.
